My coding experience

Brian Aubry

LC101 - Fall 2018

I've been using computers my whole life. I remember my dad letting me take apart one of his old computers when I was 3 or 4. I played games on the old family MS-DOS computer (Commander Keen was one of my favorites). I went from MS-DOS to Windows 3.1, to 95, to 98, to XP, on and on. I had plenty of hardware, software, networking, and troubleshooting experience. As such, I was normally the one people came to when they had issues with any piece of tech, new or old.

However, when it came to coding/programming, I had almost no experience. I had taken a basic HTML/CSS class in college, and had made a website or two on my own as a hobby. I initally used Codecademy to try to learn Python, and made it through the course in a few weeks. However, I didn't use it for a lot, tried to make a few tools for my job, and ended up kind of dropping it a few months later. I had never been in a devoted classroom atmosphere for an object oriented programming language, which I greatly desired. I learn better in a classroom, so I was just waiting for LC101 applications to open.

I am really looking forward to Unit 3. Until now, the topics we've covered in LC101 have been topics I've covered before at some point. I am hoping that I am able to join the Java portion of the class. I've never thought of myself as being too great at design, so the back-end coding has always interested me. I like making things work, finding creative solutions to problems, and sometimes just trying several different methods until something finally works. From there I can clean it up, but initially that means my code can get pretty messy at times.
